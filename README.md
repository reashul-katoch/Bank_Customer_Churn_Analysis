# Bank Customer Churn Analysis

## 🚀 Project Overview
This project analyzes **bank customer churn** to identify why customers leave and which segments are at high risk. The goal is to uncover actionable insights that help banks improve **customer retention**.

Dataset contains **10,000 customers** with attributes such as:

- Geography (Country)
- Age
- Gender
- Credit Score
- Number of Products
- Activity Status (Active/Inactive)
- Balance
- Salary
- Churn Indicator

---

## 🛠 My Role & Workflow
As the primary analyst, I performed end-to-end analysis:

1. **Data Cleaning**: Handled missing values, outliers, and data consistency.
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**: Explored trends across demographics, financial attributes, and engagement.
3. **Customer Segmentation**: Identified high-risk churn segments using multi-dimensional analysis (age, country, credit score, salary, activity).
4. **Dashboard Creation**: Built a **dynamic, interactive Power BI dashboard** with KPIs, segmentation, and risk analysis.
5. **Business Insights & Recommendations**: Suggested actionable strategies to reduce churn.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Highlights

- **Top KPIs**: Total Customers, Churn Rate, Avg Balance, Salary, Credit Score
- **Churn by Geography**: Germany shows the highest churn
- **Churn by Age**: Older customers most at risk
- **Activity Analysis**: Inactive customers contribute the most to churn
- **Credit Score & Salary Impact**: Poor credit scores and high-income customers show slightly higher churn
- **High-Risk Segmentation**: Combines country, age, activity, credit score, and salary to identify priority retention targets

---

## 💡 Key Insights

1. **Germany** has the highest churn rate (**32%**).
2. **Older and inactive customers** are most likely to churn.
3. Customers with **only one product** or **poor engagement** are high-risk.
4. Surprisingly, **high-income customers** show slightly higher churn.
5. Retention strategies should focus on **engagement, personalized products, and high-risk geographies**.
